About

I build systems where failure is not an option — from on-vehicle autonomous driving deployment at RideFlux to safety-critical visualization tools at NVIDIA.

My work spans formal safety analysis, FSM-based system design, real-time control, and humanoid robotics research. I'm drawn to problems that sit at the boundary of theory and deployment — where a clean idea has to survive contact with real hardware, real roads, and real edge cases.

I hold a B.S. in Mechanical Engineering from UC Berkeley, where I also researched robotics and vision–language models for manipulation.

Selected Projects
Safety
ISO 4804–Aligned MRM Enhancement
Replaced binary fail-safe FSM triggers with a capability-based degradation architecture. Each software component outputs a Quality Level, aggregated by a Capability Checker; a Mode Manager then adjusts the operating mode. Degradation that affects capability triggers an MRM; otherwise it's an HMI warning only — avoiding combinatorial state explosion as the ODD grows.
Functional Safety FSM MATLAB System Composer

Publications
Principal-Filter Framework for Minimal Risk Maneuvers in Autonomous Driving Systems
Yang et al. · RideFlux / UC Berkeley
A formal framework for scalable MRM design. Each MRM is defined by a Minimal Requirement Vector pk; its feasible region is a principal filter, giving monotonicity, consistency, and degrade-only structural guarantees that rule-based FSMs cannot.
